Survey: Robotic tactile perception

Shan Luo, Joao Bimbod, Ravinder Dahiyae, Hongbin Liu

Paper link

Tactile sensing modalities

  • Categories of sensors:
    1. Single point contact: force/torque sensors
    1. High spatial resolution: BioTac, GelSight, GelSlim
    1. Large-area tactile sensors: Large patches

Tactile object shape perception

  • Better shape information → better robot plans and manipulation trajectories
  • Advantages and disadvantages of vision, local vs. global sensing

Pose estimation via touch

  • Small errors in object pose can lead to incorrect placement of robot finders, affect success of grasp
  • Types of estimation:
    • Single point based: Schaeffer et al., Shape and pose recovery (Yu et al.)
      • Challenging as touch sensing is intrusive in nature, unlike vision
    • Tactile-based: using GelSight (Li et al.)
      • Vision can bootstrap initial estimate (Honda et al., Bimbo et al.)
    • Tactile-visual based: theory (Lacey et al.)
      • Probabilistic recursive Bayesian method (Luo et al.)
  • Summary:
    • Localization object during manipulation is an open problem
    • In existing SLAM methods, high accuracy and realtime performance is still desired
    • High-resolution tactile sensors can capture local shape and determine pose from few contacts

Tactile sensing in sensor fusion

  • Combining vision and touch as complementary to get the best of both worlds
  • Can also use kinesthetic cues, F/T, range sensing
  • We need a probabilistic structure that allows fusion of modalities with differing uncertainties
  • GPIS work:
    • Used in internal shape representation (Dragiev et al.)
    • iterative EKF with visual and tactile data (Ilonen et al.)
    • Visual features intialize and tactile features refine (Bjorkman et al.)

Discussion and conclusion

Object localization via touch

  • We need to move from single point sensors to tactile arrays
  • Open issues:
    • How to map sensor data to 3D space
    • How to combine data from vision and tactile
    • How to resolve conflicts amongst sensors (probabilistically?)
